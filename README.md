# Restaurant Tips Analysis (SQL Project)

## 📊 Project Description

This project analyzes restaurant transaction data to understand customer behavior, tipping patterns, and revenue trends.

## 📁 Dataset

The dataset contains 244 transactions including:

* total bill amount
* tip amount
* day of the week
* time (lunch or dinner)
* customer group size
* smoker status

## ❓ Business Questions

* When does the restaurant generate the most revenue?
* Do customers spend more during lunch or dinner?
* How does group size affect spending?
* Do smokers tip differently?
* What is the average tip percentage?

## 📈 Analysis & Insights

* The dataset contains 244 transactions with total revenue of 4,827 and an average bill of 19.7.

* Tip percentage varies across days, with higher values observed on weekends, indicating more generous customer behavior during leisure periods.

* Larger groups tend to generate higher total bills, showing that group dining significantly contributes to revenue.

* Customers spend more during dinner compared to lunch, indicating higher willingness to pay in the evening.

* The highest revenue is concentrated on weekends, especially Saturday, highlighting peak demand periods.

## 💡 Recommendations

* Increase staffing during weekends to handle higher demand.
* Focus on optimizing dinner service to maximize revenue.
* Encourage group dining promotions to increase total bill size.
* Monitor tipping patterns to better understand customer satisfaction.

## 🛠 Tools Used

* PostgreSQL
* SQL (GROUP BY, aggregations)
